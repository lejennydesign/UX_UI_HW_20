CASE STUDY


WeBoard: a collaborative moodboard


Overview

WeBoard is a visual design mood board app that draws similarities to Pinterest and Miro. From testing various mood boarding software, our team gathered various feature ideas to implement into our own app, such as looking through feeds of images based on your likes, saving images to folders, then within the folder or board being able to organize images, write notes, and share and collaborate on those boards with others.




My Role

For this project, I worked as one of the project managers and lead designers. As one of the project managers, I was responsible managing files for the team to access on our online drives and updating the Trello Board for the team’s next tasks. I also created the UI style guide and various screens in Figma for prototyping.



The Problem

We observed that designers struggle to find images shared or saved online later when they need them which is causing miscommunication or delays in their workflow. How might we… improve mood boarding so that our designers are successful based on the number of monthly users that create moodboards and upload images?


Our solution

We want to provide a flexible space to save and share photos that allows designers to organize, annotate, and collaborate with ease.




Research & Ideation




User Research & Insights

We conducted five interviews with individuals about their creative process, and we also shared a Google Form survey online to creative communities for more responses. We brainstormed various questions based on what we, as designers, would do to initiate a creative project, like how we would go about it at first and what tools we use to gather information.

Based on our interview notes, users mainly like the organization aspect of certain apps (like Pinterest and Miro) and the ability to collaborate with others.

Ultimately, we learned that a creative designer needs an efficient way to save, organize and share images and references because they juggle multiple projects that need to be thoroughly planned from start to finish and throughout this process they will need to quickly refer back to references.


User Persona

Our ideation journey started somewhere along the lines of these questions: what does the user need? Which needs are most essential? How do we build empathy towards our user’s needs? We gathered our interview and survey results and made a user persona named Iris Yam to reflect what kind of user we expect to use our app and the needs they may have while using it. Their main goals are to be able to collaborate with their partner and refer back to saved reference images later. Their pain points are having trouble exporting and sharing large files and filtering through tons of saved images.




Ideation Journey

After creating our user persona, we proceeded to make a story board about a user who uses our WeBoard concept to organize images and ideas for her creative project. We also made task user flow to represent what functions would be available in our upcoming prototype.





Prototyping




Tasks & Goals

Each team member created their own clickable prototype to achieve the tasks and goals. Our main tasks included making an easy onboarding screens, being able to make a new board in the canvas feature, the ability to import images from your camera roll and providing the means to organize and share files. We had to keep in mind that the onboarding process should be concise, the home page should be easy for users to find their boards and make new ones, making the file import feature intuitive, and the sharing options should be upfront and center.

I drew various wireframe sketches on Procreate, a digital art software. There are is an emphasis on screens of the moodboard canvas feature, as that is featured in our main user flow.




Testing & Key Takeaways

We conducted around 5-7 user tests on our paper prototypes for feedback. Here are some key takeaways:

- The onboarding process was easy on all the prototypes.
- One of the prototypes had an intuitive toolbar.
- All of the prototypes need to be developed more to use its key features (the canvas space).

Based on feedback from testing and our team votes, we iterated a low fidelity prototype with popular (and loved) features.
